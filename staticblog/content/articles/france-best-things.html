5 Best Things to See in France

1. The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is undoubtedly one of the most iconic landmarks in the world and a must-see when visiting Paris. Located in the heart of Paris, this iron lattice tower was constructed in 1889 as the entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair. Standing at 324 meters tall, the Eiffel Tower offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks. Visitors can take an elevator or climb the stairs to reach the top.

The tower is especially stunning at night when it is illuminated with thousands of sparkling lights.

Don't forget to enjoy a meal at one of the tower's restaurants for a unique dining experience.

2. The Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum, located in Paris, is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument. It is home to an extensive collection of art and artifacts, including the famous Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo. The museum is housed in the Louvre Palace, a former royal residence, and features a stunning glass pyramid entrance. With over 35,000 works of art on display, visitors can explore a wide range of exhibits, from ancient Egyptian relics to Renaissance masterpieces. The Louvre is a cultural treasure trove that offers an unparalleled experience for art lovers and history enthusiasts alike.

Checkout the official website of the Louvre Museum.

3. The Palace of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles is a symbol of the absolute monarchy of the Ancien Régime and one of the most opulent palaces in the world. Located just outside of Paris, this UNESCO World Heritage Site was the principal royal residence of France from 1682 until the French Revolution. The palace is renowned for its lavish architecture, exquisite gardens, and the Hall of Mirrors, a grand gallery adorned with 357 mirrors. Visitors can tour the royal apartments, the expansive gardens, and the Trianon estate. The Palace of Versailles offers a glimpse into the grandeur and extravagance of French royalty.

4. Mont Saint-Michel

Mont Saint-Michel is a small rocky island located in Normandy, famous for its stunning medieval architecture and picturesque setting. The island is crowned by a magnificent abbey, which dates back to the 8th century and is dedicated to Saint Michael the Archangel. Mont Saint-Michel is surrounded by tidal waters, creating a dramatic and ever-changing landscape. Visitors can explore the narrow winding streets, visit the abbey, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding bay. The island's unique beauty and historical significance make it one of France's most captivating destinations.

5. The French Riviera

The French Riviera, or Côte d'Azur, is a glamorous stretch of coastline along the Mediterranean Sea, known for its stunning beaches, luxurious resorts, and vibrant nightlife. Popular destinations along the French Riviera include Nice, Cannes, and Saint-Tropez. Visitors can relax on the sandy beaches, stroll along the Promenade des Anglais in Nice, or attend the prestigious Cannes Film Festival. The region is also home to charming villages, such as Èze and Saint-Paul-de-Vence, which offer a more tranquil and picturesque experience. The French Riviera is the perfect destination for those seeking sun, sea, and sophistication.

France is a country rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. From the iconic Eiffel Tower to the enchanting Mont Saint-Michel, there is no shortage of incredible sights to see. Whether you're an art lover, history buff, or simply looking to relax on the beautiful beaches of the French Riviera, France has something to offer every traveler. These five destinations are just a glimpse of the many wonders that await you in this captivating country.
