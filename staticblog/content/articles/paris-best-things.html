5 Best Things to See in Paris

1. The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is undoubtedly the most iconic landmark in Paris and a must-see for any visitor. Constructed in 1889 as the entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair, this iron lattice tower stands at 324 meters tall. Visitors can take an elevator or climb the stairs to reach the observation decks, which offer breathtaking views of the city.

The tower is especially stunning at night when it is illuminated with thousands of sparkling lights.

Don't forget to enjoy a meal at one of the tower's restaurants for a unique dining experience.

2. The Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris. Housed in the Louvre Palace, a former royal residence, the museum features a stunning glass pyramid entrance. The Louvre's extensive collection includes over 35,000 works of art, ranging from ancient Egyptian relics to Renaissance masterpieces. Highlights include the Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo, and the Winged Victory of Samothrace. Art lovers and history enthusiasts can spend hours exploring the museum's diverse exhibits, making it a cultural treasure trove.

The Louvre Museum is on iconic place and it is one of the best places to visit in France, not only in Paris.

3. Notre-Dame Cathedral

Notre-Dame Cathedral is a masterpiece of French Gothic architecture and one of the most famous cathedrals in the world. Located on the Île de la Cité in the heart of Paris, the cathedral is renowned for its stunning facade, intricate sculptures, and beautiful stained glass windows. Visitors can explore the interior of the cathedral, climb the towers for a panoramic view of Paris, and visit the crypt to learn about the history of the site. Despite the devastating fire in 2019, restoration efforts are underway to preserve this iconic landmark for future generations.

4. Montmartre and the Sacré-Cœur Basilica

Montmartre is a historic and artistic district in Paris, known for its bohemian atmosphere and charming streets. The area is home to the Sacré-Cœur Basilica, a stunning white-domed church that sits atop the highest point in the city. Visitors can climb the steps to the basilica for a breathtaking view of Paris or take a leisurely stroll through the winding streets of Montmartre. The district is also famous for its vibrant arts scene, with numerous galleries, studios, and the iconic Place du Tertre, where artists display their work.

5. The Champs-Élysées and Arc de Triomphe

The Champs-Élysées is one of the most famous avenues in the world, stretching from the Place de la Concorde to the Arc de Triomphe. Lined with shops, cafes, theaters, and gardens, the avenue is a bustling hub of activity and a popular destination for both locals and tourists. At the western end of the Champs-Élysées stands the Arc de Triomphe, a monumental arch that honors those who fought and died for France during the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars. Visitors can climb to the top of the arch for a stunning view of the city and the surrounding avenues.

Champs-Élysées has the best shopping in Paris, so you have to allow enough time to walk this beautiful street.

Paris is a city of unparalleled beauty, history, and culture. From the iconic Eiffel Tower to the charming streets of Montmartre, there is no shortage of incredible sights to see. Whether you're an art lover, history buff, or simply looking to experience the magic of the City of Light, Paris has something to offer every traveler. These five destinations are just a glimpse of the many wonders that await you in this enchanting city.
